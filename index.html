D1
June 9 — Arrive & Straight to Santorini
Athens Piraeus → Santorini ferry
Ferry tip: Book a morning Blue Star departure from Piraeus (~7:30am). Land in Athens, take metro to Piraeus (~45 min), board ferry, arrive Santorini by mid-afternoon. It's a big travel day but you wake up on the island on your birthday!
Flight lands
Arrive Athens ATH. Metro Line 3 to Monastiraki, then Line 1 to Piraeus port (~45 min total, €9/person). Head straight to Gate E6/E7.
Ferry departs
Blue Star Ferry from Piraeus to Santorini. ~7–8 hr crossing. Grab a sun deck spot — the Aegean scenery is spectacular. Pack snacks or use the onboard café.
Late afternoon
Arrive Athinios Port, Santorini. Pre-book a private transfer to Perissa (~€35–45 for the car — no direct bus from Athinios to Perissa). Check in, freshen up. First sunset on the island!
Evening
Beachfront dinner in Perissa. Fresh grilled fish and local Assyrtiko wine. You made it to Santorini — toast to that.
Birthday — June 10!
D2
June 10 — Birthday in Santorini
Perissa beach + Oia sunset
Morning
Wake up on the black sand beach. Swim in the Aegean, lie in the sun. This is your day — no rush. Breakfast at a beachfront café with fresh fruit, yoghurt and honey (€8–10).
Midday
Rent an ATV/scooter (€25–35/day for two) and drive up to Ancient Thira — a 4th-century BC city perched on Mesa Vouno mountain above Perissa. Incredible views over both coasts.
Afternoon
Drive to Fira (the capital) for a wander. Explore the caldera edge, have a coffee overlooking the volcano. Then head to Oia — the iconic white-and-blue village at the island's northern tip.
Birthday sunset
Watch the sunset from Oia's castle ruins — one of the most famous sunsets in the world. Arrive by 7pm to get a spot. The sky turns gold and pink over the caldera. Unforgettable birthday moment.
Birthday dinner
Splurge on a birthday dinner in Oia or Fira with caldera views. Budget €30–45/person for a special meal with wine. You've earned it.
D3
June 11 — Akrotiri & Wine
Archaeology + Santorinian wine tasting
Morning
Akrotiri — Santorini's "Minoan Pompeii." A 3,600-year-old Bronze Age city preserved under volcanic ash. One of Greece's most important archaeological sites. Bus from Fira (€2) or drive. Entrance €15/person.
Midday
Red Beach — a 5-minute walk from Akrotiri. Dramatic red and black volcanic cliffs meeting the sea. Unique and stunning. Grab lunch at a nearby taverna.
Afternoon
Wine tasting at Santo Wines or Venetsanos Winery — both have caldera views and Santorini's famous Assyrtiko white wine. A tasting flight is €12–18/person and includes jaw-dropping views.
Evening
Back to Perissa for a relaxed dinner and beach bar drinks. Watch the stars over the Aegean.
D4
June 12 — Fira & Last Beach Day
Explore the capital, afternoon on Perivolos
Morning
Spend the morning in Fira — walk the caldera path, visit the Museum of Prehistoric Thera (€6), and browse the local shops. Grab a coffee on the cliffside.
Afternoon
Last beach afternoon at Perivolos (the southern, more upscale extension of Perissa). Lounge, swim, and enjoy the black sand one last time. Some beach clubs have free sunbeds if you order drinks.
Evening
Dinner in Perissa village — fresh seafood meze platter and local wine at a harbour-side taverna. Early night — ferry to Athens tomorrow morning.
D5
June 13 — Ferry back to Athens
Santorini → Piraeus, settle into Athens
Morning
Morning ferry from Athinios port back to Piraeus. Take the bus from Perissa to Fira, then connecting bus to the port (allow 90 min + early arrival at port). Or book private transfer (€35).
Afternoon
Arrive Piraeus. Metro to Monastiraki. Check into Athens hotel. Drop bags and head to the Monastiraki flea market — great souvenir shopping and people-watching.
Evening
First real evening in Athens. Dinner in Psiri neighbourhood — one of Athens' best areas for affordable, authentic food. Try lamb souvlaki or moussaka (€12–15/person at local spots).
D6
June 14 — Acropolis Day
Athens' ancient heart
Early morning (8am)
Beat the heat and the crowds — arrive at the Acropolis gates when they open. The €30 combo ticket covers 7 sites: Acropolis, Agora, Olympieion, Kerameikos, and more. See the Parthenon in morning light.
Late morning
Acropolis Museum (€10/person) — stunning modern building showcasing the original Parthenon sculptures. One of the best museums in Europe. Air-conditioned escape from midday heat.
Afternoon
Plaka neighbourhood — Athens' oldest and most charming area, winding lanes below the Acropolis. Lunch at a shaded taverna. Browse shops for olive oil, ceramics, and local products to bring home.
Evening
Sunset drinks at a Monastiraki rooftop bar with Acropolis views. Then dinner in the neighbourhood — gyros, Greek salad, and cold Mythos beer.
D7
June 15 — Cape Sounion & Fly Home
Morning excursion, afternoon flight
Morning
Early KTEL bus to Cape Sounion (€8/person, 2 hrs each way) — the Temple of Poseidon perched on dramatic cliffs over the Aegean. Lord Byron carved his name in the stone. Arrive before the heat peaks.
Midday
Back to Athens by early afternoon. Light lunch near the hotel. Collect bags, check out.
Afternoon/Evening
Metro Line 3 from Monastiraki to the airport (€9/person, ~40 min). Evening flight home to Toronto. Safe travels — what a birthday trip!

Perissa is the smartest budget choice on Santorini — pool, steps from the beach, good restaurants nearby, and 40–60% cheaper than caldera-view hotels in Fira/Oia. Smaragdi is consistently rated one of the best budget picks. Also check: Meltemi Village, Aqua Blue Hotel, or Perissa Bay. Book via Booking.com with free cancellation. Target rooms under €90/night (private double).

Athens (Piraeus) ↔ Santorini — Ferries
Blue Star Ferries — recommended
7–8 hrs · Stable, spacious, open decks · Departs ~7:30am from Piraeus (Gate E6–E8)
~€57–70/person one way (~$85–105 CAD)
Book a Numbered Seat (not deck class) for ~€15 more — worth it for the comfort on a 7-hr crossing. Book both directions now on Ferryhopper.com.

  • !
    Book the ferry before the hotel. June Blue Star morning sailings from Piraeus to Santorini sell out. This is your most time-sensitive booking. Go to Ferryhopper.com today.
  • !
    Port transfer in Santorini is tricky. There are no direct buses from Athinios port to Perissa. Book a private transfer in advance (~€35–45/car). Do not count on finding a taxi at the port in June.
  • $
    Perissa vs Oia accommodation. A hotel in Perissa costs 40–60% less than caldera-view hotels in Oia or Imerovigli. You still get Santorini — just with better beaches and a lower bill. Day-trip to Oia for the sunset.
  • $
    Oia sunset — arrive by 7pm. The prime sunset viewpoint at Oia Castle fills up 45–60 minutes before sunset in June. Get there early and find your spot. It's free, but crowded — go on a weekday if possible.
  • i
    Rent an ATV, not a car. An ATV for two costs €25–35/day and is perfect for Santorini's roads. Much cheaper than a car rental, easy to park, and more fun. Bring sunscreen — there's no shade on the road.
  • $
    Athens €30 combo ticket. Covers 7 sites including the Acropolis, Ancient Agora, Temple of Zeus, and Kerameikos. Buy it at the first site you visit — it's valid for 5 days.
  • i
    Airport bus in Athens. Metro Line 3 from the airport to city centre costs €9/person. Taxis run €40+. The metro is fast (40 min) and reliable — use it both ways.
  • $
    Free sunbeds in Perissa. Many beach tavernas offer free sunbeds if you order food or drinks from them. Saves €10–20/day vs. renting sunbeds at a beach club.
  • !
    Heat tip. In June, midday temperatures hit 30°C+. Do outdoor sightseeing before 11am or after 5pm. Carry water everywhere. The black sand in Perissa gets scorching — always wear flip-flops to the water.
  • i
    Travel insurance. With two ferry journeys involved (which can be cancelled due to Aegean winds), insurance is strongly recommended. Budget ~$80–120 CAD total for two people. Use a Canadian provider like Manulife or Allianz.
